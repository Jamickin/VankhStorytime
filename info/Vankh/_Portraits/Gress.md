## Ready-to-paste prompt

Gress — a polished Cicillian executive, composed and elegant, with terror
flickering just beneath the surface. TWO CANON FEATURES THAT MUST BE PRESENT:
(1) she is completely hairless — a bare, smooth skull, no hair, no eyebrows; (2)
her eyes are bi-pupiled — EACH eye holds two distinct dark pupils set side by
side within a single iris. Cold, refined, precise features. At her temples, sleek
chrome neural enhancement ports gleam, catching cold light in faint prismatic
reflections across her face — painted as part of her, elegant, not a device. She
wears pristine, expensive corporate attire, too new and too perfect. Her
composure is cracking: her enhanced eyes are dilating wide with the first edge of
panic, dignity barely holding, a sheen of cold sweat at her brow. Cold
ultramarine and steel-blue light from one side; the rest falling into deep
shadow, with a single faint warm ember at the very edge.

STYLE BLOCK — Painterly Expressionism in the spirit of Disco Elysium's character
portraits (Aleksander Rostov): a loose, gestural OIL PAINTING where the features
are SUGGESTED, not described — edges dissolve into the brushwork, forms melt at
their boundaries, whole passages of the face left unresolved and indistinct.
Thick impasto and dragged palette-knife strokes beside thin scumbled paint;
visible canvas tooth. Melancholic, psychologically heavy, dignified yet worn.
Muted, desaturated palette — umber, ash-grey, cold teal shadow — broken by a few
resonant accents of cadmium red and ochre in the flesh. Atmospheric chiaroscuro;
the figure emerges from a dark, abstract painted field. This is emotionally
evocative FINE ART, not illustration — it should make the viewer feel something
before they understand anything. Hand-painted, imperfect, alive. Square 1:1.

Avoid: any hair, eyebrows, or stubble; normal single-pupil eyes; glossy chrome
cybernetics or overt sci-fi tech; clean digital rendering, smooth airbrushed
skin, sharp photographic detail; anime or cartoon styling; a tidy, fully-resolved
"corporate concept-art" look; text or watermarks.

## Why these choices

- Painted at the moment her control fails (the edge of Dilation Hysteria on the
  raw shuttle) — cold polish with fear breaking through is the feeling, not a
  composed executive headshot.
- Cold ultramarine/steel light + the chrome temple-ports set her in deliberate
  contrast to Pro's warmth and grief; the single warm ember keeps the gallery
  palette cohesive.
- The chrome is kept painterly ("part of her, not a device") so it reads as
  character, not sci-fi gadgetry — feeling leads.
