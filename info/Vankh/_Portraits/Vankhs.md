# Portrait prompt — The Vankhs (Vankh spheres / the Vankh's Eye)

For Gemini (image generation). This is a **landscape / cosmic object**, no
figure. Generate **square, 1:1**. Save to `static/lore/vankh-spheres.png`, then
set `image: "/lore/vankh-spheres.png"` on the `vankh-spheres` entry in
`src/lib/content/lore.js`.

Keep the **STYLE BLOCK** verbatim (identical to the portraits) so the gallery
reads as one painter's hand. Lead with the core canon: a perfect black sphere,
the Eye at its centre, the Pulse radiating out.

---

## Ready-to-paste prompt

The Vankhs — a colossal, flawless sphere of perfect, absolute black hanging in
the deep of space, so dark it swallows the surrounding starlight and bends it
around itself. It is matte and depthless: not a planet, not a machine, but pure
void given form — no surface, no texture, no technology, only impossible
blackness. At its heart is the Vankh's Eye, a deeper darkness within the dark, a
pupil of nothing that seems to hold unfathomable depth, as though it opens onto
other realities. From the sphere the Pulse radiates outward — vast concentric
shockwave rings rippling through the void, smearing and bending the distant
stars into trembling halos, a silent thunder made visible. Threaded faintly
through the distortion, barely perceptible, are spirals of uncanny mathematical
order — golden-ratio curves, the ghost of a hidden design beneath a natural
facade. Cold ultramarine and viridian light pools where space ripples; faint
embers of cadmium and ochre flare along the tears in reality. Far below, almost
nothing, a single tiny shuttle drifts — a fleck of warm light, dwarfed to
insignificance. The mood is the sublime and the dreadful at once: sacred,
watchful, alive with an intelligence that should not be there.

STYLE BLOCK — Painterly Expressionism in the spirit of Disco Elysium's character
portraits (Aleksander Rostov): a loose, gestural OIL PAINTING where the forms
are SUGGESTED, not described — edges dissolve into the brushwork, shapes melt at
their boundaries, whole passages left unresolved and indistinct. Thick impasto
and dragged palette-knife strokes beside thin scumbled paint; visible canvas
tooth. Melancholic, psychologically heavy, vast. Muted, desaturated palette —
umber, ash-grey, cold teal shadow — broken by a few resonant accents of cadmium
red and ochre. Atmospheric chiaroscuro; the subject emerging from a dark,
abstract painted field. This is emotionally evocative FINE ART, not illustration
— it should make the viewer feel awe and dread before they understand anything.
Hand-painted, imperfect, alive. Square 1:1.

Avoid: a textured planet or moon; glossy CGI or sci-fi render; a photographic
space/telescope photo; lens flares; pretty nebula stock art; any visible
machinery or technology on the sphere; clean digital smoothness; cartoon or
anime styling; text or watermarks. The sphere must be perfectly, flatly black —
not a glowing star, not a textured world.

---

## Why these choices

- The sphere is the universe's hidden author (revealed in Chapter 1.3) — so it's
  painted as the *sublime*: awe + dread, sacred and wrong, watching back. That's
  the feeling, not "a cool space rock."
- The faint golden-ratio spirals seed the Chapter 1.3 revelation (impossible
  mathematical precision beneath a "natural" facade) without spelling it out.
- The tiny shuttle is optional but gives the scale that makes it overwhelming,
  and supplies the one warm accent against all that black.
- Perfect flat black is the hardest ask (models love to make it a glowing star
  or a textured planet) — it's stated twice and in the avoid-list. Generate a
  few and pick the blackest, emptiest one.
