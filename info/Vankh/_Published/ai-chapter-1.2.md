# Chapter 1.2: The Transit

"Everything flows, out and in; everything has its tides; all things rise and fall."

---

The [[Vankh Pulse]] thundered through the cabin, a vibration that seemed to resonate in the marrow of [[Pro]]'s bones. The sound was more felt than heard—a deep harmonic that bypassed the ears and spoke directly to cellular structures, to the enhanced nervous systems that had been carefully calibrated to survive this violation of natural law.

_Right lung. Left lung. Left lung. Right lung._

[[Pro]] reversed his breathing pattern with practiced precision, easing his body back into the rhythm that would carry him safely through the transition. Around him, passengers gasped and clutched their restraints as the vessel's reality alignment fought to stabilize. The independent shuttle lacked the sophisticated dampening systems of corporate vessels, making every phase of transition a brutal reminder of the forces they routinely defied.

Outside the viewport, space itself began to fragment in ways that challenged human perception. The black void between stars took on texture—rippling patterns that most passengers were trained to ignore, but that [[Pro]] had always found fascinating. These distortion fields resembled the Vankh spheres themselves—perfect black orbs that dotted settled space, emitting the pulses that made faster-than-light travel possible.

Reality stretched. The familiar sensation washed over him like being pulled in all directions simultaneously while remaining perfectly still. His specialized training combined with the neural calibrators at his temples allowed him to adjust to the temporal distortion, his enhanced bi-pupiled eyes filtering the visual anomalies that poured through the viewports—streaking stars and fluctuating color spectrums that could drive the unprepared mad.

But something was different this time.

Beyond the standard visual distortions of FTL travel, [[Pro]] noticed something unusual in the void—a secondary ripple pattern that seemed to echo the primary pulse. In his eight [[Vankh-Sett]]s of transit work, he'd never observed this phenomenon. It resembled the harmonic undertones he sometimes detected in poorly calibrated couplings, but occurring in space itself rather than mechanical systems.

_What the hell?_

Before he could analyze the anomaly further, a strangled gasp from across the aisle pulled his attention back to the cabin's crisis. The corporate woman—[[Gress]]—was struggling with the reality shift, her breathing pattern faltering as panic overtook her training. A sheen of sweat glistened on her forehead, and her chromed eyes had dilated fully, reflecting the chaotic light patterns in terrifying amplification.

The early symptoms of [[Dilation Hysteria]] flickered across her features as her perception destabilized—the past and present beginning to blur, her enhanced visual cortex overwhelmed by temporal displacement effects. Her vision would be swimming now, cascades of shapes flowing across her eyes, prior conversations replaying as both déjà vu and memory simultaneously.

Around them, other passengers were faring poorly. The [[Purist]] adherents suffered worst of all—the elderly man with the religious medallion had gone completely rigid, his eyes rolled back showing only whites. The crude scar tissue where he had removed his neural stabilizers left him particularly vulnerable to transit effects. A transit attendant moved efficiently through the cabin, administering emergency stabilizers to those showing the most severe symptoms.

But [[Gress]] was approaching the critical threshold. [[Pro]] could see it in the way her hands trembled, the rapid flutter of her enhanced eyelids as temporal echoes cascaded through her visual field. The worst would come next—vertigo, followed by the violent Snap-Back that could leave permanent neural damage.

"Amateur," [[Pro]] whispered, keeping his eyes fixed on her distress. Without conscious thought, he began tapping his foot against the deck plates, creating a rhythm she could follow. The metallic _click-click-click_ cut through the disorienting hum of the drives, offering a lifeline of pattern in the chaos of transitional space.

The sound reached her through the temporal confusion. Her breathing began to stabilize, following his lead as her enhanced nervous system grasped onto the external rhythm. [[Pro]] maintained the steady beat, watching as the panic gradually receded from her features.

Just before the Snap-Back could take hold, her vision cleared. The temporal displacement faded, her chromed implants cycling back to normal operational parameters. After a tense moment that seemed to stretch eternally, the shuttle shuddered one final time as reality fully transitioned to FTL state.

The view outside the viewport had transformed completely. No longer the star-filled blackness of normal space, FTL transit created its own unique visual environment—a swirling tunnel of blue-shifted light at the periphery surrounding an absolute void at the center. Corporate scientists called it the "transit aperture," but technicians like [[Pro]] knew it by a different name: the Vankh's Eye.

The perfect darkness at the tunnel's center seemed to possess depth beyond measurement, as though it opened onto spaces that existed outside normal dimensional relationships. Legend among drive workers claimed that staring too long into this central void could reveal glimpses of other realities—or drive the viewer permanently mad.

Advanced corporate shuttles prevented such episodes with sophisticated stabilization systems—lights that pulsed in gentle guidance, ceiling displays providing visual synchronization cues, hull stabilizers that dampened the worst of the transition effects. This independent vessel offered none of these luxuries, just raw transition that separated the experienced from the novice with brutal efficiency.

"Thank you," [[Gress]] whispered, her voice unsteady as corporate composure temporarily abandoned her. "I don't usually... that is..."

"No shame in it," [[Pro]] replied, his tone neither patronizing nor particularly sympathetic. "Corporate shuttles have stabilization systems. Indies run raw. First time's always rough."

He glanced around the cabin, noting that several passengers had not weathered the transition well. Two [[Purist]] adherents were still receiving emergency medical attention, their consciousnesses temporarily displaced by [[Dilation Hysteria]]. The religious man with the medallion sat with glazed eyes, technically conscious but experiencing the profound temporal dissociation that often triggered conversion to Purist ideology. Many of the [[Prophet]]'s most fervent followers had first encountered his teachings while in this vulnerable state.

Within moments, [[Gress]] slipped into the coma-like state that most passengers entered during deep FTL travel—a protective mechanism of the mind against the fundamental wrongness of faster-than-light existence. Her enhanced systems would maintain basic life functions while her consciousness retreated from the impossible reality of their situation.

Around the cabin, other passengers were following the same pattern. The successful transition had drained their nervous systems, and now they sought refuge in unconsciousness while the shuttle crossed distances that should have taken decades in mere hours. The few who remained awake were either extensively modified for transit work, like [[Pro]], or suffering from enhancement failures that prevented proper neural shutdown.

With most passengers unconscious and the cabin finally quiet, [[Pro]] found himself oddly alert. Usually, he prioritized rest during these trips to compensate for his chronic sleep deficiency, but something felt fundamentally wrong. The drive's hum carried an unfamiliar note, a barely perceptible dissonance that shouldn't exist in a properly aligned system.

He closed his eyes, focusing his specialized hearing on the mechanical symphony beneath the deck plates. The independent shuttle's coupling system should produce a specific harmonic signature—the carefully calibrated dissonance that propelled them across vast distances by opposing the natural Vankh resonance.

But there it was again—that secondary pattern, like an echo resonating through his chest cavity with unsettling precision. Not a mechanical issue such as loose components vibrating against the hull, but something more fundamental. Something in the pulse itself.

The anomaly he'd glimpsed during transition hadn't been his imagination. Whatever was generating that secondary ripple pattern in space was also affecting the drive's harmonic output. The implications were troubling—if external forces were influencing their FTL systems, the carefully calibrated balance that kept them alive during transit could be compromised.

[[Pro]] opened his eyes, studying the perfect darkness of the Vankh's Eye beyond the viewport. The absolute void seemed to pulse with its own rhythm, separate from but related to the mechanical harmonics of the shuttle. For a moment, he could swear he detected a pattern in that darkness—a subtle variation that matched the secondary echo he'd been hearing.

_Professional paranoia,_ he told himself, but his enhanced nervous system continued to register the anomaly. Years of working with drive systems had given him an almost supernatural sensitivity to harmonic irregularities. When something was wrong with a coupling, he could feel it in his bones long before his instruments confirmed the problem.

And right now, every instinct was screaming that something was very wrong indeed.

The shuttle continued its journey through the impossible geometry of FTL space, its passengers unconscious and vulnerable while cosmic forces beyond their understanding carried them toward their destination. Only [[Pro]] remained awake and aware, his technical training and enhancement-modified senses detecting patterns that others couldn't perceive.

He felt for his tool bag, fingers finding the familiar weight of his diagnostic equipment. The portable scanners could analyze the shuttle's harmonic output, providing hard data about the anomalies his senses detected. But using them during FTL transit violated several safety protocols—the sensitive instruments could be damaged by the exotic energies surrounding them, and their electromagnetic emissions might interfere with the delicate balance that kept them alive.

Yet the secondary pattern was growing stronger, more pronounced with each passing moment. Whatever was causing it seemed to be building toward some kind of threshold, and [[Pro]] found himself increasingly convinced that understanding its nature might be crucial for their survival.

The darkness beyond the viewport pulsed again, and this time he was certain—the rhythm matched the harmonic echo perfectly, as though the void itself was responding to their passage through it. Or perhaps calling to something within their vessel, something that was beginning to answer.

[[Pro]]'s hand closed around his diagnostic scanner. Safety protocols were important, but not as important as understanding what was happening to their reality during transit. The other passengers slept on, unaware that their journey through impossible space was revealing patterns that challenged everything they thought they knew about the nature of existence itself.

The Vankh's Eye stared back at him, its perfect darkness pregnant with mysteries that demanded investigation. And deep in the shuttle's drive systems, something was beginning to sing a song that shouldn't exist—a harmony that spoke of forces beyond human comprehension, reaching across the void to touch their small vessel with purposes unknown.

[[Pro]] made his decision. Regulations be damned—he needed to know what was happening.

His fingers found the scanner's activation control.
