# Chapter 2.2: The Correspondence

"As above, so below; as below, so above."

---

"Technician P-R-0-4-7-9." [[Lin]] came forward out of the tunnel mouth, and the mathematical enhancements beneath her skin resolved into a living grid—matrices flowing across her temples like equations rewriting themselves, neural pathways brightening as they computed the optimal distance from which to conduct a conversation. "I am Supervisor L-I-N-3-8-2. Your oversight appointment has been advanced to immediate compliance."

[[Pro]] wiped his hands on a maintenance cloth, his interfaces quietly saving his diagnostic progress. "I'm in the middle of a critical [[drive coupling]] repair, Supervisor. Leave it half-finished and the drive could fail in transit."

"The repair may continue under supervision." [[Lin]] settled into a position that maintained perfect sightline on his hands. "Corporate policy now requires direct oversight of all independent-contractor activity. Implemented three cycles ago. A priority area for quality assurance and security compliance."

He returned to the coupling, hyperaware of the augmented perception cataloguing every movement—every tool drawn, every reading taken. Her systems were measuring him against corporate standard, and where he deviated, her calculation arrays flared.

"Your methodology is interesting," she observed, and the word in her mouth was not a compliment. "You compensate for harmonic drift using non-standard resonance patterns."

"Independent vessels require improvisation." [[Pro]] eased a crystalline matrix back into true with a pressure measured in fractions. "Corporate procedure assumes optimal components. Indie operators work with what they can afford."

"Deviation from established procedure introduces unpredictable variables into system performance."

"Sometimes unpredictable variables are what keep the system alive."

Her enhancements pulsed—disapproval, or its mathematical equivalent—and then her attention shifted to his tools, and [[Pro]] felt the first real flicker of unease. The scanners and resonators spread across his cloth were his own work, years of small improvements layered one over the next: enhanced sensitivity, broader spectrum, pattern-recognition tuned for configurations no corporate manual acknowledged. Capabilities, in plain fact, that exceeded his authorized enhancement level.

"Your equipment shows non-standard modification," [[Lin]] said, neural pathways flaring as she read the specifications straight off the housings. "These capabilities exceed standard issue for your specialization."

The conversation had walked itself to the edge of a cliff. Those tools were the whole of his independent work, and his independent work was the whole of his parents' medicine. "Necessary adaptations for diverse equipment," he said carefully. "The modifications were performed by licensed specialists using approved components. All of it documented to regulation." Which was true, in the way that a wall is true while the door behind it stays shut.

Her arrays cycled through their analysis, decision matrices brightening and resolving. "Documentation will be reviewed," she concluded at last, the patterns settling toward a temporary equilibrium. "Continue your work."

He finished the repair over the next two hours under the unblinking weight of her attention, restoring the coupling to function if not to grace. The captain would have to keep his acceleration reduced and his schedule honest, but the immediate crisis was past. [[Pro]] logged the work to the maintenance database and felt her cross-reference every line of it in real time.

"Your work meets minimum standards despite methodological irregularities," [[Lin]] said. "However. Your independent activities require ongoing supervision. Report for oversight briefing before accepting any further contracts. Administrative Level Seven, Office 247-B." The mathematical patterns made it plain the instruction was not open to negotiation. Then she withdrew with precise efficiency, her enhancements already turning toward whatever the next task on her ledger required.

[[Pro]] watched her go. Through the entire exchange her systems had never once relaxed into the easier rhythms he saw in other corporate citizens. She existed in a state of perpetual calculation, as if human contact itself were a problem to be optimized. He filed her, too, the way he had filed the scarring on the hull: another piece of a pattern he did not yet have the shape of.

The captain reappeared once she was gone. "That one's been everywhere indies work. Documented every off-books operation in the sector these past three cycles." His crystalline patterns dimmed back toward their resting glow. "Word is corporate analysis flagged 'security concerns' with independent contractors. Could be political. Could be economic. Could be both."

It could also, [[Pro]] thought, be neither—could be one more instrument in a system that measured its subjects from every possible angle. But he only secured his tools and made the captain the offer that mattered: "If you need emergency work again, contact me directly. Not through corporate channels."

"Same to you, if you ever need transport that doesn't ask questions. We remember the ones who help."

The underground medical facility kept to the oldest sections of [[Nexus Station]], where the original construction had been added to and added to across generations. The architecture here remembered a different age—less segregation, more integration, technologies and approaches from every faction sharing the same cramped corridors. A relic of a time when cooperation had outweighed purity. [[Pro]] entered through a nutrition dispensary front, his technical credentials a plausible reason for a man to be standing where he stood, and the bell triggered a scan that read him as neither corporate enforcement nor [[Purist]] zealot.

The back room was the [[Mechanic]]'s true workshop—a clinical space that would have shamed a good many corporate clinics, advanced diagnostics sharing bench room with traditional synthesis equipment, a hybrid that served clients from every faction without preference. The [[Mechanic]] herself stood at the far end of the integration spectrum, her original evolved features all but vanished beneath enhancement systems built for function and indifferent to the face they had replaced.

"The [[coupling]] technician returns," she said, her voice braided with the harmonic overtones of vocal modification. "Another delivery for the family?"

[[Pro]] set his badge on the scanner, opening his credit balance to her. "The usual package. Plus temporal stabilizers, if you have them."

Her enhancement patterns—far more intricate than [[Lin]]'s clean mathematical grids—flowed through diagnostic sequences. "Temporal degradation. The progressive kind. Cortical implants in rejection." It was not a question. "They're expensive. Developed for deep-space miners living under chronic dilation. Four thousand credits."

More than his independent work would earn him in months. He thought of his mother's eyes, clearer for a single dose, telling him the medicine tasted like sunlight. "Add them to the package."

As she assembled the smuggling container—shaped and labeled to read as maintenance supplies under a casual scan—[[Pro]] caught his reflection in the polished face of a cabinet. His evolved anatomy and his corporate modifications met seamlessly in the glass: the elongated digits, the enhanced senses, the neural ports, all of it integrated into a single working whole. The [[Purist]]s would call it corruption and cut it out of him without anesthetic, to teach him purity. [[Cicillian]] would call it progress and sell him more of it, and quietly record what it learned. Two doctrines pulling their civilization apart along the same seam, and neither of them, he was beginning to suspect, asking the question that mattered.

"You should think about ending these visits," the [[Mechanic]] said, sealing the container, and the concern in it sounded genuine. "Oversight is tightening. [[Purist]] space grows less safe for the enhanced by the cycle."

"Family doesn't recognize political boundaries."

She pressed the package into his hands, her systems settling toward standby. "The universe operates on many rhythms, technician. The [[Vankh pulse]] is merely the most obvious."

The heresy hung in the air between them, casual and enormous. That the rhythm beneath every life they knew might be only the loudest layer of something deeper. A [[Vankh-Sett]] ago he would have let it pass as a strange old woman's strange old talk. Now it settled against the scarred hull and the impossible couplings and his mother's unmatched archives, and it fit.

His quarters in the corporate residential ring received him with the seamless attention he had stopped being able to ignore. The lighting adjusted to his profile, the atmosphere to his lungs, the ambient hum to a frequency tuned—he understood now—to entrain his circadian cycle to the station's master rhythm. Data streams flowed across every surface, updates and notifications and invitations, an environment built to enhance his life and, in the same motion, to watch it. For the first time he found himself wondering what was being collected. What his conversations, his pauses, his physiological responses were worth, and to whom, and where the record of them went when it left him.

He hid his parents' medicine in a cavity beneath the maintenance panel, using corporate technology to conceal the cost of corporate policy, and lay down on a sleeping platform whose systems were already preparing to guide his consciousness through optimized rest. Even sleep, here, was mediated—steered through predetermined patterns by machines that loved him exactly as much as a process loves its inputs.

_As above, so below,_ he thought as the lights dimmed themselves. The coupling and the cosmos. The hull and the horizon. The hidden infrastructure and the seamless world it carried. The patterns at the smallest scale and the patterns at the largest were the same patterns, and once a man had learned to read one of them he could not stop reading the rest. Correspondence, level answering level, all the way up and all the way down.

[[VVVVVANKH!]]

The pulse rolled through the quarters with perfect mechanical timing, and [[Pro]] lay still in the dark and listened past it—past the primary rhythm to the secondary harmonic beneath, the patient half-beat answer that had followed him out of transit and would not be put down. It did not drive the pulse. It _replied_ to it. Something on the far side of the barrier, tapping back through the wall.

He had begun to hear the real music under the surface melody. And once heard, he already knew, it could never again be unheard.
