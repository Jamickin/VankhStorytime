# Chapter 1.3.1: The Crystal

Around him the cabin stirred. Restraint clips snapped open. The automated systems nudged nervous systems back toward the present. Someone three rows forward was already checking their implant status against the docking manifest, already resuming.

[[Pro]] did not move.

He reached into his tool bag.

The [[data crystal]] was warm, which it should not have been. Passive storage ran cold — an inert lattice, no current, no heat signature. He turned it over in his palm, watching the purple glow pulse faintly behind its shielded casing in a rhythm he had never noticed before. A rhythm that matched nothing in the official specifications.

_Left lung. Right lung._

He exhaled. Unlocked the casing.

The archives opened in his enhanced vision as dense overlays, text unspooling from a structure so deep it took his calibrated systems a moment to parse the indexing scheme. His [[Pro's Mother]] had not been keeping notes. She had been keeping evidence.

Population logs that didn't align with the census projections by orders of magnitude — not computational error, but systematic reduction, consistent across every region and every decade. Stellar cartography discrepancies filed and then refiled, each revision nudging the settlements slightly closer together, the empty distances between them slightly smaller. Mission logs from vessels that had traveled what the records called light-years, returning with mineral samples indistinguishable from the nearest surveyed region.

The archives didn't prove the experiment. They proved the correction. Someone — something — was maintaining the illusion not perfectly but diligently. Adjusting the numbers. Filing away the anomalies. Smoothing the edges of a box drawn around them all, generation by generation.

She had found the seam. That was what her failing implants had given her — not degradation, but an unfiltered angle on a managed reality. The neural calibration arrays that normally kept human perception on the approved frequency had simply stopped doing their job, and she had looked through the gap.

The docking tone sounded. [[Pro]] sealed the crystal and returned it to his bag. His hands were steady. He had always been able to keep his hands steady when it mattered.

He joined the disembarking queue. Around him, passengers stretched and consulted their implants and composed themselves for the business waiting at [[Nexus Station]]. He moved with them, carrying a weight of knowledge that had no good destination and no safe harbour.

But it was his now. He would not put it down.
